Social Whispers

Project from 2020

In times of crisis, such as the COVID 19 pandemic, the crucial role of social networks becomes clear - especially when it comes to communication and information. But not every user has good intentions. "Social Whispers" aims to draw attention to this problem and encourage users to deal with content in social media in a reflective and critical manner. "Social Whispers" is intended to create a basis for future action in social media. In the simulation, users slip into the role of a person who has lost their memory. In search of their identity, they explore the world of "Social Whispers" and leave their mark. Every decision has consequences that become clear in the course of the simulation. In the end, users learn which Internet persona they embody in the process.

During the course of the project, I mainly took on tasks in conception and programming in JavaScript.

Guia

Project from 2020/21

Finding your way around a train station is not always easy. Under time pressure or due to inaccurate signage, you can end up on the wrong track. Even for sighted people, it is not easy to maintain an overview and orientation. For blind people, however, this is an even bigger problem. "GUIA" is an interactive medium that enables blind people to find their way around the train station independently by using tactile sense instead of visual sense. The prototype is a fanny pack with vibration motors. These are located around the waist of the blind person and can guide the person through the station using specific patterns.

My focus in this project was on team organization, target group research, programming the prototype in JavaScript (p5.js, node.js), C, HTML and CSS, conceptual design in general and conceptual design of the project presentation.

FOPO

Project from 2020/21

"FOPO" is an acronym for "Food Portfolio" and was created as part of the elective "Society Centered Design". We addressed the question of how to better provide (initial) access to the topic of balanced and sustainable nutrition not only to nutrition experts and young adults studying the topic, but also to the general society, and especially to teenagers and children.

As a solution to this problem, we have chosen an approach that takes place in the children's immediate environment - at school. Through weekly challenges, students learn how to use specific ingredients and special diets (vegan, vegetarian, low-carb, etc.). Ingredients will be locally sourced, as students will also be taught about sustainability. Challenges are to be completed in a small team and recreated at home with family or friends.

School of Compassion

Project from 2020/21

As part of the Systemic Compassion elective, we looked at how to build more compassion between two parties. In our case, we focused on the problem of compassion between teachers and students. As the project progressed, we followed the Double Diamond design process model.

Our result is a small card game that can be played in class as needed to give and receive feedback or to reinforce lesson material in a fun way. One of the 25 cards is randomly drawn at the beginning of the feedback lesson. How long the game lasts depends on the card. With this little game, we want to lower the inhibition threshold to give and receive criticism and ensure better cooperation in the classroom. My tasks were to research the target group and the conception of the game

Best Beer

Singleproject from 2020

"Best Beer" is a small AR app written in the Swift programming language. Therefore, it runs exclusively on IOS devices. The project was developed in individual work as part of the elective "Mobile Programming". The concept behind the app is that users can enter their local beer into the app and give a rating. All users of this app can now scan the bottle in the app the next time they buy beer and see all the ratings for that beer. Now you can send any of your friends out to buy new beer without worrying that they will get the wrong one.

Discord-Bot

Mini-Project from 2021

For a small submission in the subject "Coding" I have worked with the Tenor API, Discord API and RandomFacts API. The whole project was written in JavaScript. The bot responds to various commands, which can also be requested via the "!help" command. For example, a gif can be output via the "!gif" + search term command. Via the "!lol" command, a random fun fact with matching gif is output.
